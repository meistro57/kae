**Emergent concepts:**
- Why is it so tough to understand the Holy Bible? (weight: 3.1)
- [ANOMALY] Mainstream academic biblical scholarship (historical-critical method) and mainstream theological hermeneutics (doctrinal/systematic theology) operate in a fundamental contradiction. The former treats the text as a composite human document to be dissected, insisting meaning is *behind* the text in its historical genesis. The latter often treats the text as a transparent vessel of divine propositional truth, where meaning is *in front* of the text in its doctrinal application. Both sideline the possibility that the Bible's "toughness" stems from it being a **relational, self-referential, and ontologically enacting system**—akin to Rosen's (M,R)-system (closed to efficient cause) or an apparatus of agential cuts (Barad) that enacts a specific world (Mol). The silence is on developing a hermeneutic that takes the text as a *causal, world-constituting agent* rather than a passive object of study or a simple container of messages. This forces either reduction or dogma, missing the relational core that produces the interpretive difficulty. (weight: 1.6) ⚠
